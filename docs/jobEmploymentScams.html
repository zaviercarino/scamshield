Job & Employment Scams

Home / Library / Job & Employment Scams

What It Is

Scammers create fake job listings to steal your money or personal information. These offers often promise remote work, flexible hours, or easy pay. After a quick "interview," they may send fake checks or request sensitive details, leaving victims with lost money or stolen identities.

⚠️ Warning Signs

Common Red Flags:

  • Interview is text/chat only: no video or call
  • Hired instantly, no screening
  • Sent a check, asked to return part of it
  • Asked to pay upfront for training or equipment
  • SSN or bank info requested before a formal offer
  • High pay for vague, minimal-effort work

How to Stay Safe

  • Search "[company name] + scam" before applying anywhere
  • Never deposit a check and wire money back, even if your bank shows it as available
  • Insist on a real video interview before accepting an offer
  • Never pay out of pocket for training, equipment, or background checks
  • Don't share your SSN or bank info until you've signed a verifiable offer letter
