Online Shopping Scams

Online shopping scams involve fake stores, fraudulent listings, and deceptive sellers who trick customers into paying for products that never arrive or are significantly different from what was advertised.

Scammers often create professional-looking websites, steal product photos, and advertise unrealistic deals to convince shoppers to make a purchase.

How Online Shopping Scams Work

🛒

The Fake Store

Scammers create fake websites, social media shops, or listings that appear to sell popular products.

🏷️

The Too-Good Deal

They advertise extremely low prices, limited-time offers, or rare products to pressure buyers into purchasing quickly.

📦

The Fake Delivery

After payment, the product never arrives, tracking information is fake, or the item received is completely different.

The Catch

They use fake reviews, copied product images, and professional-looking websites to appear like a legitimate seller.

The Scam

After receiving payment, scammers may disappear, send a fake product, or refuse refunds and customer support.

Protect Yourself

Browse the manual below to learn tips on how to stay safe.

Warning Signs / Red Flags
  • Prices are much lower than other websites
  • The website was recently created or has little information
  • The seller has few reviews or only suspicious reviews
  • Product photos appear copied from other websites
  • The website has poor grammar or unusual formatting
  • The seller pressures you to buy quickly
  • Payment methods do not offer buyer protection
  • There is no real customer service information
  • The product descriptions seem unrealistic or exaggerated
  • The seller refuses to answer questions about the product
How to Stay Safe
  • Research the seller and read reviews from multiple sources
  • Buy from trusted websites and official stores
  • Be cautious of deals that seem too good to be true
  • Use payment methods with buyer protection
  • Check website information before making purchases
  • Avoid clicking shopping links from unknown messages
  • Compare prices with other legitimate sellers
  • Look for secure payment options and contact information
  • Do not share unnecessary personal information
  • Save receipts and order confirmations
What To Do If You've Been Scammed
  • Contact your payment provider or bank immediately
  • Request a refund or dispute the transaction
  • Save order details, emails, and conversations with the seller
  • Report the fake website or seller platform
  • Change passwords if an account was compromised
  • Monitor your accounts for suspicious activity
  • Report the scam to consumer protection agencies
  • Be careful of fake refund or recovery scams
Quick Checklist
  • Have I researched this seller before buying?
  • Are the prices unusually low compared to other stores?
  • Does the website have real contact information?
  • Are there trustworthy reviews from multiple sources?
  • Am I being pressured to buy immediately?
  • Does the payment method offer buyer protection?
  • Do the product photos and descriptions seem legitimate?
  • Would I trust this store if I found it randomly online?
