Job & Employment Scams

Job scams are fraudulent employment offers designed to steal your money, personal information, or identity. Criminals often pretend to represent legitimate companies and advertise attractive positions with high salaries, remote work, or easy hiring.

Their goal isn't to employ you—it's to convince you to send payments, share sensitive information, or install malicious software.

How Job Scams Work

🪧

The Fake Opportunity

The victim sees a job posting through a job board, email, text message, or social media.

💰

Fast Hiring

The "employer" quickly contacts the applicant and often skips a proper interview.

💼

Building Trust

They use company logos, professional emails, and convincing documents to appear legitimate.

The Catch

They request payment for training, equipment, certifications, or background checks—or ask for banking information before employment.

The Scam

Once the victim pays or shares sensitive information, the scammer disappears or continues asking for more money.

Protect Yourself

Browse the manual below to learn tips on how to stay safe.

Warning Signs / Red Flags
  • Offers a high-paying job with little experience required
  • Hires you immediately without a proper interview
  • Requests payment for training, equipment, or application fees
  • Asks for personal information before you are officially hired
  • Uses unofficial email addresses or suspicious contact methods
  • Pressures you to accept the offer quickly
  • Communicates only through text or messaging apps
  • Sends fake checks and asks you to send money back
  • Requires you to purchase equipment from a specific vendor
  • The job description contains vague details or unrealistic promises
How to Stay Safe
  • Research the company using official websites and trusted sources
  • Verify recruiter emails and contact information
  • Never pay money to apply for or receive a job
  • Avoid sharing sensitive personal information too early
  • Be cautious of offers that seem too good to be true
  • Request a proper interview before accepting a position
  • Do not deposit checks or send money on an employer's request
  • Confirm job offers directly with the company
  • Take your time and avoid rushed decisions
  • Report suspicious job postings or recruiters
What To Do If You've Been Scammed
  • Stop communicating with the scammer immediately
  • Contact your bank or payment provider if money was sent
  • Change passwords if any account information was shared
  • Monitor your accounts for suspicious activity
  • Report the scam to the platform where you found the job
  • Document messages, emails, and payment details as evidence
  • Notify relevant authorities or consumer protection agencies
  • Warn others about the scam to help prevent further victims
Quick Checklist
  • Did I apply for this job or did they contact me first?
  • Was there a real interview process?
  • Does the recruiter use a legitimate company email?
  • Did I research the company independently?
  • Am I being asked to pay any fees?
  • Does the salary match the job requirements?
  • Am I being pressured to decide quickly?
  • Am I being asked to share sensitive personal information?
