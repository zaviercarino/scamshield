Gift Card Scams

Gift card scams are fraud attempts where criminals trick victims into purchasing gift cards and sending the codes or information from them. Scammers often pretend to be trusted organizations, businesses, or people in need of urgent help.

Their goal is to turn gift cards into untraceable payments. Once the scammer receives the gift card details, the money is usually gone and difficult to recover.

How Gift Card Scams Work

📞

The Fake Request

The scammer contacts the victim pretending to be a company, government agency, family member, or someone needing help.

🎁

The Gift Card Demand

They pressure the victim to buy specific gift cards and send the card numbers or photos of the codes.

💸

The Payment

After receiving the codes, scammers quickly redeem the balance and may continue asking for more money.

The Catch

They create panic or urgency by claiming there is an emergency, unpaid bill, account issue, or consequence if you do not pay.

The Scam

Once the gift card information is shared, the scammer takes the money and disappears or demands additional payments.

Protect Yourself

Browse the manual below to learn tips on how to stay safe.

Warning Signs / Red Flags
  • Someone demands payment using gift cards
  • They create a sense of urgency or panic
  • They ask you to read gift card numbers or send pictures of codes
  • They claim to be from a company, government agency, or support team
  • They threaten consequences if you do not pay immediately
  • They tell you not to tell anyone about the payment
  • They request unusual payment methods instead of normal options
  • They ask for multiple gift cards or increasing amounts of money
  • They contact you unexpectedly about an issue you did not know about
  • The situation feels rushed or suspicious
How to Stay Safe
  • Never use gift cards to pay bills, fines, or unexpected fees
  • Ignore anyone who demands gift card payments
  • Verify requests by contacting the organization directly
  • Do not share gift card numbers or security codes
  • Take time to think before making urgent payments
  • Talk to friends or family if a request seems suspicious
  • Remember that legitimate companies rarely request gift cards
  • Report suspicious messages or calls
What To Do If You've Been Scammed
  • Contact the gift card company immediately
  • Keep the gift card and purchase receipts
  • Report the scam to the platform where contact happened
  • Contact your bank if additional payments were involved
  • Change passwords if personal information was shared
  • Save messages, emails, and payment details as evidence
  • Report the scam to the appropriate fraud reporting agency
  • Be cautious of follow-up scammers offering recovery help
Quick Checklist
  • Am I being asked to pay with a gift card?
  • Did I receive an unexpected request for payment?
  • Am I being pressured to act immediately?
  • Is someone asking for gift card numbers or codes?
  • Did I verify the request through an official source?
  • Would a legitimate company ask for this payment method?
  • Am I being told to keep the payment secret?
  • Does this situation feel unusual or suspicious?
