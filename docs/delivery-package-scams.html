Delivery & Package Scams

Delivery and package scams are fake messages posing as shipping companies. They use tracking updates, missed delivery notices, or fee requests to trick you into clicking malicious links or sharing payment and personal information.

Their goal is to exploit the fact that many people are expecting online orders, making fake delivery notifications seem believable.

How Delivery & Package Scams Work

📦

The Fake Delivery Message

The victim receives a text, email, or message claiming there's an issue with a package.

❗

Creating Urgency

The message warns that the package cannot be delivered unless immediate action is taken.

🌐

Fake Tracking Website

The victim is directed to a fake website that closely resembles a legitimate shipping company.

Information or Payment Request

The scammer asks for personal information, login credentials, or a small delivery fee.

The Scam

The victim loses money, exposes sensitive information, or unknowingly installs malware.

Protect Yourself

Browse the manual below to learn tips on how to stay safe.

Warning Signs / Red Flags
  • Unexpected delivery notifications for packages you didn't order
  • Messages claiming your package is delayed or cannot be delivered
  • Requests to pay small shipping, customs, or redelivery fees
  • Suspicious tracking links or shortened URLs
  • Poor spelling, grammar, or generic greetings
  • Pressure to act immediately before the package is returned
  • Requests for payment information or personal details
  • Tracking numbers that don't work on official shipping websites
  • Messages from unfamiliar phone numbers or email addresses
  • Links directing you to websites that don't match the shipping company
How to Stay Safe
  • Track packages only through official shipping websites or apps
  • Do not click links in unexpected delivery messages
  • Verify tracking numbers directly with the shipping company
  • Be cautious of requests for small delivery or customs fees
  • Check whether you're actually expecting a package
  • Keep your devices and browsers updated
  • Never enter payment information on suspicious websites
  • Delete suspicious texts and emails without interacting
  • Contact the shipping company using official contact information if unsure
What To Do If You've Been Scammed
  • Contact your bank or credit card provider immediately if you made a payment
  • Change passwords if you entered login credentials on a fake website
  • Monitor your financial accounts for unauthorized activity
  • Save screenshots, messages, and payment confirmations
  • Report the scam to the shipping company being impersonated
  • Report suspicious text messages or emails to your mobile carrier or email provider
  • Run a security scan if you downloaded any files
  • Report the scam to the appropriate authorities or consumer protection agencies
Quick Checklist
  • Am I actually expecting a package?
  • Does the tracking link match the official shipping company's website?
  • Am I being asked to pay an unexpected delivery fee?
  • Does the message create unnecessary urgency?
  • Is the sender's email address or phone number legitimate?
  • Did I verify the tracking number independently?
  • Does the website look secure and official?
  • Have I confirmed the delivery issue before taking action?
