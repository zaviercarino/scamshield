Cryptocurrency & Investment Scams

Cryptocurrency and investment scams promise quick, guaranteed, or unusually high returns to convince victims to invest money that ultimately disappears.

Scammers often impersonate financial experts, successful investors, or cryptocurrency platforms. They may build trust over days or weeks before encouraging victims to invest, only to steal the money or prevent withdrawals.

How Cryptocurrency & Investment Scams Work

💬

The Opportunity

The scammer contacts you through social media, messaging apps, dating apps, or email with an exciting investment opportunity.

📈

The Fake Profits

They show fake account balances, fabricated profits, or testimonials to convince you that your investment is growing.

💸

The Theft

When you try to withdraw your money, you're asked to pay additional fees—or the scammer disappears entirely.

The Catch

They create excitement by promising guaranteed profits, insider opportunities, or investments with little or no risk.

The Scam

Victims transfer money or cryptocurrency into accounts controlled by scammers, making recovery extremely difficult.

Protect Yourself

Learn how legitimate investments work and recognize the warning signs before sending money.

Warning Signs / Red Flags
  • Promises of guaranteed or risk-free returns
  • Pressure to invest immediately
  • Unexpected investment offers from strangers
  • Requests to pay using cryptocurrency
  • Claims that only a limited number of people can invest
  • Profits that seem unrealistically high
  • Being asked to pay taxes or fees before withdrawing money
  • Unlicensed brokers or unknown investment platforms
  • Testimonials or success stories that cannot be verified
  • Refusal to answer detailed questions about the investment
How to Stay Safe
  • Research every investment before sending money
  • Be skeptical of guaranteed returns
  • Verify that investment companies are properly registered
  • Never invest because someone pressures you to act quickly
  • Avoid sending cryptocurrency to strangers
  • Seek advice from trusted financial professionals
  • Independently verify investment opportunities
  • Be cautious of unsolicited investment messages
  • Understand how an investment works before investing
  • Trust your instincts if something feels too good to be true
What To Do If You've Been Scammed
  • Stop sending money immediately
  • Contact your bank or payment provider
  • Report the investment platform if applicable
  • Save transaction records, wallet addresses, and messages
  • Report the scam to local authorities
  • Warn friends or family if the scam spread through social media
  • Change passwords if financial accounts were involved
  • Ignore anyone claiming they can recover your money for a fee, as this may be another scam.
Quick Checklist
  • Am I being promised guaranteed profits?
  • Am I being pressured to invest quickly?
  • Have I independently researched this investment?
  • Is the company properly registered?
  • Am I being asked to send cryptocurrency?
  • Do the promised returns seem realistic?
  • Can I verify the person contacting me?
  • Does this opportunity sound too good to be true?
