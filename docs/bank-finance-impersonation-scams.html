Bank & Financial Impersonation Scams

Bank and financial impersonation scams occur when criminals pretend to be trusted institutions, like your bank or payment service, to steal money or sensitive information. They create urgency with fake fraud alerts or account issues and pressure victims through calls, texts, emails, or websites to share security codes, click links, or send money.

Always verify unexpected financial messages directly through your bank's official app, website, or phone number before taking action.

How Finance Scams Work

🏦

The Fake Alert

The scammer claims there is fraud, suspicious activity, or an issue with your financial account.

📞

Impersonating Trust

They pretend to work for your bank, a fraud department, or financial service.

⚠️

Creating Urgency

They pressure you to act quickly by making the situation seem serious.

The Request

They ask for passwords, security codes, card details, or money transfers.

The Scam

Once they get access or payment, they steal funds or sensitive information.

Protect Yourself

Browse the manual below to learn tips on how to stay safe.

Warning Signs / Red Flags
  • Unexpected calls, texts, or emails claiming to be your bank.
  • Pressure to act immediately.
  • Requests for passwords, PINs, or one-time verification codes.
  • They ask you to move money to a "safe account."
  • Links that don't match your bank's official website.
  • Caller ID matches your bank but something feels off.
  • Threats that your account will be frozen if you don't act.
  • Requests for gift cards, cryptocurrency, or wire transfers.
How to Stay Safe
  • Never click links from unexpected banking messages.
  • Open your bank's official app or type the website yourself.
  • If you receive a call, hang up and call the number on the back of your card.
  • Never share passwords, PINs, or one-time security codes.
  • Enable transaction alerts and multi-factor authentication.
  • Take your time—real banks won't pressure you to act immediately.
What To Do If You've Been Scammed
  • Contact your bank immediately and report the fraud.
  • Freeze or lock affected cards and accounts.
  • Change your online banking password and enable MFA if you haven't already.
  • Monitor your accounts for unauthorized transactions.
  • Report the scam to the FTC or your country's fraud reporting agency.
  • If personal information was stolen, consider placing a fraud alert or credit freeze with the credit bureaus.
Quick Checklist
  • Banks won't ask for your password or verification code.
  • Ignore urgent requests to move money to a "safe account."
  • Never trust caller ID alone—it can be spoofed.
  • Visit your bank through its official app or website.
  • Contact your bank using a trusted phone number if you're unsure.
