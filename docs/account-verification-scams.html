Account Verification & Phishing Scams

Account verification and phishing scams use fake emails, text messages, and websites that imitate trusted organizations to steal passwords, verification codes, and personal information.

Scammers often pretend to be banks, social media platforms, delivery companies, or online services to trick victims into clicking fake links or providing sensitive information.

How Account Verification & Phishing Scams Work

📧

The Fake Message

The scammer sends an email, text, or notification pretending to be from a trusted company.

🔗

The Fake Link

They send a link to a fake website designed to look like a real login or verification page.

🔑

The Information Theft

Once the victim enters their information, scammers use it to access accounts or steal identities.

The Catch

They create urgency by claiming your account is locked, compromised, or requires immediate verification.

The Scam

After collecting passwords, verification codes, or personal details, scammers use the information for unauthorized access.

Protect Yourself

Browse the manual below to learn how to identify phishing attempts and protect your accounts.

Warning Signs / Red Flags
  • The message claims your account needs urgent verification
  • You are asked to click a suspicious link
  • The sender uses a strange email address or number
  • The website URL does not match the official organization
  • You are asked for passwords or verification codes
  • The message creates fear or pressure to act quickly
  • There are spelling mistakes or unusual formatting
  • The company asks for information they normally would not request
  • You receive an unexpected security alert
  • The message encourages you to bypass normal login methods
How to Stay Safe
  • Do not click links from unexpected messages
  • Visit websites by typing the official address yourself
  • Never share passwords or verification codes
  • Check the sender's email address carefully
  • Enable multi-factor authentication on important accounts
  • Verify suspicious messages through official support channels
  • Keep devices and security software updated
  • Use unique passwords for different accounts
  • Be cautious of urgent account warnings
  • Report phishing messages when possible
What To Do If You've Been Scammed
  • Change affected passwords immediately
  • Enable multi-factor authentication
  • Contact the organization involved
  • Sign out of suspicious account sessions
  • Monitor accounts for unusual activity
  • Report the phishing message or website
  • Check for unauthorized account changes
  • Save messages and evidence of the scam
Quick Checklist
  • Did I expect this account verification message?
  • Is the sender actually from the official organization?
  • Am I being asked to click a link or log in?
  • Does the website URL look legitimate?
  • Am I being asked for a password or verification code?
  • Is the message creating unnecessary urgency?
  • Did I verify the request through an official source?
  • Would I normally receive this type of notification?
