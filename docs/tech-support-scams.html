Tech Support Scams

Tech support scams are fraudulent attempts where criminals pretend to be from trusted companies or security teams. They claim your device has a virus, security issue, or account problem and pressure you into paying for unnecessary services or giving them access to your computer.

Their goal is to create fear and urgency so victims act before verifying whether the warning is real.

How Tech Support Scams Work

⚠️

The Fake Warning

The victim receives a pop-up, phone call, email, or message claiming their device has a serious problem.

❗

Creating Panic

The scammer warns that personal files, accounts, or financial information are at risk.

🧑‍💻

Fake Support

The scammer pretends to be a legitimate company representative and offers to "fix" the issue.

Remote Access Request

They ask the victim to install remote access software or share sensitive information.

The Scam

The scammer charges fake repair fees, steals information, or continues requesting payments.

Protect Yourself

Browse the manual below to learn tips on how to stay safe.

Warning Signs / Red Flags
  • Unexpected pop-ups claiming your device is infected or compromised
  • Messages or calls claiming to be from Microsoft, Apple, or another trusted company
  • Pressure to act immediately or risk losing your data
  • Requests to install remote access software
  • Requests for passwords, security codes, or payment information
  • Asking to pay for unnecessary repairs or security services
  • Fake phone numbers displayed in pop-up warnings
  • Scammers asking you to keep the conversation secret
  • Unprofessional messages, spelling mistakes, or suspicious links
  • Claims that normal device warnings require immediate paid assistance
How to Stay Safe
  • Do not call phone numbers shown in unexpected pop-ups
  • Close suspicious browser windows instead of interacting with them
  • Contact companies through their official websites
  • Never allow strangers remote access to your device
  • Do not share passwords or security codes
  • Keep your operating system and security software updated
  • Be cautious of unexpected calls about device problems
  • Ask someone you trust if a warning seems suspicious
  • Use trusted security tools to scan your device
What To Do If You've Been Scammed
  • Disconnect your device from the internet if remote access was given
  • Remove any remote access software installed by the scammer
  • Change passwords for affected accounts
  • Contact your bank if payments or financial information were shared
  • Run a security scan on your device
  • Review your accounts for suspicious activity
  • Save screenshots, messages, and payment records
  • Report the scam to the appropriate platform or authorities
Quick Checklist
  • Did I receive this warning unexpectedly?
  • Is someone pressuring me to act immediately?
  • Did I contact support myself through an official website?
  • Am I being asked to install remote access software?
  • Am I being asked for passwords or security codes?
  • Does the warning contain suspicious links or phone numbers?
  • Am I being asked to pay for an urgent "fix"?
  • Have I verified the problem with a trusted source?
